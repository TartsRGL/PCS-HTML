Content Structure & Hierarchy

This article explains how structured headings create meaningful and scalable documents.

What Is Content Hierarchy?

Content hierarchy defines the logical order of information on a page.

Why It Is Important

Proper hierarchy improves clarity, accessibility, and maintainability.

Tip

Avoid skipping heading levels. Always follow logical document order.

Published in PCShtml

Why Hierarchy Matters

Clear hierarchy ensures predictable reading and navigation structure.

Readability

Structured headings guide the reader through the content naturally.

Accessibility

Screen readers rely on consistent heading levels to navigate the document.

Note

Assistive technologies depend on consistent heading structure.
